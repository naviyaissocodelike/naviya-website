# The Vision

## 2036 (Age 37)

Be someone governments, companies, and investors call when they need to figure out how AI should change their rules, funding, or structure. Have built or shaped at least one institution (DA or beyond) that demonstrably changed how capital flows to AI. Have a body of work (writing, advising, investing track record) that proves I understand both the technology and the systems around it.

The through-line: this isn't about making AI smarter. It's about redesigning the human systems around intelligence -- how capital flows to it, how institutions govern it, how people access its upside.

## 2031 (Age 32)

Have a portfolio of 50+ angel investments with early returns proving my judgment. DA runs independently with 1,000+ members and a track record LPs or institutional partners take seriously. Known well enough that founders and policymakers reach out to me, not the other way around. Financially independent enough to walk away from any single role.

## The Role I Want to Play

I want to sit at the intersection of three things that rarely overlap in one person: deep understanding of frontier AI, credibility as an investor and institution-builder, and proximity to the policy decisions that shape how AI gets deployed. The combination matters because the biggest gaps in AI right now aren't technical. They're institutional. Capital allocates to AI poorly. Government adopts it slowly. The people building the next wave of AI infrastructure (decentralized compute, agentic systems, AI-native financial rails) are disconnected from the people regulating it. I want to be a bridge across those gaps.

That means two things in practice:

**Influencing good AI** by being close enough to frontier development to understand what's real, what's hype, and what's dangerous. Not as a researcher, but as someone who can translate between builders, capital, and institutions. Writing about it. Building frameworks. Developing the kind of taste for AI applications that comes from seeing hundreds of pitches, reading the research, and understanding deployment in practice.

**Opening investment opportunities** by building DA into the institution that gives emerging investors access to the best early-stage deals in AI and crypto, with the tools and education to evaluate them. DA is the vehicle for a broader thesis: that angel investing is broken for everyone except insiders, and that AI-native workflows can fix that.

## The Dual-City Strategy

**DC** is where AI meets government, defense, and cybersecurity. Federal AI spending hit $7.2B in obligated funds in 2026 (up 966% from 2024), and the DoD alone requested $13.4B for AI and autonomous systems. DC is the 12th best startup ecosystem globally, and the corridor from DC to NYC holds 30% of all AI startups. Being in DC means proximity to the companies building AI for the most consequential use cases: national security, critical infrastructure, intelligence. It means access to the policy conversations that will determine how AI gets regulated, exported, and deployed by the largest buyer in the world.

**SF** is where AI meets crypto and the frontier. It has the highest density of AI PhDs per square mile on earth, 57% of all US venture capital, and the epicenter of the AI x crypto convergence. 40% of all crypto VC in 2025 went to AI-integrated projects. The decentralized compute networks (Render, Akash, io.net), the agentic economy infrastructure, the tokenized AI models -- this is all happening in SF. Being there means being in the room where the next financial and computational infrastructure gets designed.

The split isn't about hedging. Each city feeds a different part of the thesis. DC feeds the "influencing good AI" side: understanding how the largest institutions in the world adopt and govern AI. SF feeds the "opening investment opportunities" side: being at the frontier of AI x crypto, where the next generation of investable infrastructure is being built.

## How DA Fits

DA is not a side project. It's the institutional expression of the entire thesis. The question "how do we give more people access to leverage?" runs through everything: access to deal flow, access to education, access to the tools that make smart investing possible without an existing network.

By 2028: 500+ angels, 30-40 portfolio companies, AI-powered diligence workflows that make the community less dependent on any single person. Every quarter, the right question is: what became less dependent on me?

## The Five Assets

Everything backchains to building five things simultaneously:

1. **Expertise** in frontier AI deployment, governance, and the AI x crypto intersection
2. **Network** of founders, operators, investors, and policymakers across DC and SF
3. **Reputation** as a recognized voice at the intersection of AI, capital, and institution design
4. **Wealth** through startup ownership, personal investing track record, and optionality
5. **Institution** (DA) that compounds independently of individual effort

The H2 2026 OKRs are about accelerating all five at once.
